Computer Science vs Web Development

Something I took a lot of consideration into before going to college was what type of degree I want to go for. First and foremost I wanted it to be something that could increase my job prospects. Next, is something that I can see myself doing for some sort of amount of time. Lastly, something that can funnel into my passion for helping others. This landed me somewhere with technology, coding, and electricity. More specifically, coding.

Web Development

After researching and looking up a lot of stuff about what programmers are and what they do, I landed at my local college for a web development program. I knew that this left me plenty of options and control, which is part of it that I liked. Unlike Computer Science, you don't have to get a degree to land certain jobs in the field. Web development works with websites, that's it. It's a skill that can be learned in various ways; self-taught, boot camp, and school.

Web development is more focused on getting you to create useful websites than teaching you more overarching concepts about coding. You do learn as you go about some of those concepts but dive specifically into them. If web development was building a house, it would be like choosing the siding and internal furnishings rather than laying the house, which computer scientists normally handle.

Computer Science

Computer science is more than web deve nlopment, an analogy is that computer scientists can be web developers, but web developers cant be computer scientists. This isn't to belittle web developers, but rather is an uncomfortable truth, at least for beginners like me.
