Assignment 8

The first activity was pages about the different ways to be safe on the Internet. I learned that being safe on the Internet isn't limited to a few things, but is multifaceted and complex. There are hundreds of ways to secure your accounts, data, and general computer well being. It can start with the wi-fi, the software you enabled on your computer, and what websites you access. I think that the analogy used that was very useful is "treat the Internet like a mall, you wouldn't hand your information over to someone you don't trust.". I think this is important int he future because knowing many of the vulnerabilities that users or your self may run into, will allow for more preparation and counteractive measures.

The fourth activity was researching password managers and multi-factor authentication methods. I ended up choosing and using LastPass, mainly because of its robust free version. I learned that other premium programs often come packed with other features such as VPNs, cloud storage, and other monitoring features to keep users safe. Personally, in the future, this will allow me to chose a product that will meet all my needs and keep me safe on the Internet.

The fifth activity was the final activity I did. The page led to a test of your network and the possible vulnerabilities that it has. After running some tests I learned that the most dangerous part of my network is how the UPnP for the router may allow some ports to remain open. On my personal computer, the firewall and other features Windows disables appears to be keeping my data safe. In the future, I will use a test like this to see the vulnerabilities of private networks I might connect to on a regular basis such as a friend's home. I will be able to tell them of these vulnerabilities and hopefully offer some ways to defend themselves.
